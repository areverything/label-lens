## Evidence

Aspartame (E951, CAS 22839-47-0) is authorized as a sweetener in the European Union with an acceptable daily intake (ADI) of 40 mg/kg body weight per day (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II). In the United States, it is permitted as an approved general-purpose sweetener (21 CFR 172.804). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The safety evidence indicates that the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has established an ADI of 40 mg/kg body weight per day (EFSA re-evaluation, http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2013.3496). Additionally, the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies aspartame as 'possibly carcinogenic to humans' (Group 2B) (IARC/JECFA joint statement (Jul 2023). It is important to note that a hazard classification or being banned in some jurisdictions does not equate to being proven harmful.
