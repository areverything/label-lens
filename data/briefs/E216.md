## Evidence

Propylparaben (E216, CAS 94-13-3) is not authorized as a food additive in the European Union, as its authorization was withdrawn in 2006 (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 (not listed)). In California, propylparaben is banned from food effective 2027 (California AB 418 (2023)). The regulatory statuses differ between these jurisdictions, with the EU having withdrawn its authorization and California implementing a future ban.

There is no safety evidence on record for propylparaben, including an acceptable daily intake (ADI) from EFSA or a classification from IARC. It is important to note that being banned in certain jurisdictions or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
