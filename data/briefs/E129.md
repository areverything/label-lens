## Evidence

Allura Red AC (FD&C Red 40) is regulated differently in the European Union and the United States. In the European Union, it is authorized but requires a warning label indicating it may have adverse effects on activity and attention in children (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex V). In contrast, the United States permits its use as a listed color additive (FD&C Red No. 40) without such warnings (21 CFR 74.340). There is no regulatory status provided for other jurisdictions, indicating that data is not yet compiled.

The safety evidence includes a re-evaluation by the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), which is documented (https://efsa.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.2903/j.efsa.2015.4007). However, no specific Acceptable Daily Intake (ADI) or IARC classification is mentioned in the provided facts. It is important to note that being banned in some jurisdictions or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
