## Evidence

Tartrazine (FD&C Yellow 5) is regulated differently in the European Union and the United States. In the European Union, it is authorized but requires a warning label stating it "may have an adverse effect on activity and attention in children" (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex V). In contrast, in the United States, it is permitted as a listed color additive (FD&C Yellow No. 5), with certification required (21 CFR 74.705). There is no information provided regarding its status in other jurisdictions, so that data is not yet compiled.

The safety evidence includes a re-evaluation by the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), which is documented (http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2009.1331). However, there is no indication of an IARC classification or an established acceptable daily intake (ADI) provided in the facts. It is important to note that a regulatory status of being banned or classified as a hazard does not equate to being proven harmful.
