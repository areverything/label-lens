## Evidence

Indigotine (FD&C Blue 2) is authorized as a food color in the European Union under Annex II conditions, and no child-attention warning is required (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II). In the United States, it is permitted as a listed color additive (FD&C Blue No. 2), but certification is required (21 CFR 74.102). There is no information provided regarding its regulatory status in other jurisdictions, so that status is not yet compiled.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has established an acceptable daily intake (ADI) for Indigotine at 5 mg/kg body weight per day (EFSA re-evaluation, https://efsa.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.2903/j.efsa.2014.3768). There is no IARC classification provided for Indigotine, which means that there is no established cancer hazard classification. It is important to note that being banned in some jurisdictions or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
