## Evidence

Sodium nitrate (E251) is authorized as a preservative in the European Union, with maximum added amounts being lowered by Regulation (EU) 2023/2108, effective from October 2025 (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II (amended by Reg (EU) 2023/2108)). In the United States, it is permitted as a preservative and color fixative in cured fish and meat-curing preparations, with a limit of up to 500 ppm (21 CFR 172.170). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has established an acceptable daily intake (ADI) for sodium nitrate at 3.7 mg/kg body weight per day (EFSA re-evaluation, https://efsa.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/epdf/10.2903/j.efsa.2017.4787). The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies ingested nitrate or nitrite, under conditions that result in endogenous nitrosation, as probably carcinogenic (group 2A); however, this classification pertains to specific exposure conditions rather than the additive itself (IARC Monograph Vol. 94). It is important to note that a hazard classification is not the same as being proven harmful.
