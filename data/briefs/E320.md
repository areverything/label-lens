## Evidence

Butylated hydroxyanisole (BHA) is listed under Proposition 65 in California as a carcinogen (OEHHA Prop 65 list (BHA)). The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies BHA as possibly carcinogenic to humans (Group 2B) (IARC Monograph Vol. 40). There is no information provided regarding its regulatory status in other jurisdictions, so that data is not yet compiled.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has conducted a re-evaluation of BHA, but specific details regarding an acceptable daily intake (ADI) are not provided in the facts. It is important to note that being classified as a possible carcinogen or being banned in certain jurisdictions does not equate to being proven harmful.
