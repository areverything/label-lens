## Evidence

Acesulfame potassium (E950) is authorized as a sweetener in the European Union, subject to conditions outlined in Annex II of Regulation (EC) 1333/2008. In the United States, it is permitted as a general-purpose sweetener and flavor enhancer in foods, with the exception of meat and poultry (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II; 21 CFR 172.800). There is a divergence in regulatory status between the EU and the US, as the EU imposes specific conditions for its use while the US allows broader application.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has conducted a re-evaluation of Acesulfame potassium, but specific details regarding an Acceptable Daily Intake (ADI) or IARC classification are not provided in the facts. It is important to note that being banned in some jurisdictions or classified as a hazard does not equate to being proven harmful (http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2016.4437).
