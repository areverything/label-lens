## Evidence

Potassium nitrite (E249) is authorized as a preservative in the European Union, with maximum added amounts being lowered by Regulation (EU) 2023/2108, effective from October 2025 (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II, amended by Reg (EU) 2023/2108). In the United States, it is permitted as a prior-sanctioned color fixative and preservative in the curing of red meat and poultry (21 CFR 181.34). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies ingested nitrate or nitrite, under conditions that result in endogenous nitrosation, as probably carcinogenic (group 2A) (IARC Monograph Vol. 94). However, this classification pertains to specific exposure conditions and does not indicate that the additive itself is proven harmful. There is no safety evidence on record regarding potassium nitrite.
