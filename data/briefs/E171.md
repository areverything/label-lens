# Titanium dioxide (E171, CAS 13463-67-7)

## Identity
- **Name:** Titanium dioxide
- **E-number:** E171
- **CAS:** 13463-67-7 (also 1309-63-3)
- **Family:** colour

## Regulatory status

| Jurisdiction | Status | Detail | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| European Union | banned | Banned as a food additive; no longer considered safe (EFSA 2021 could not exclude genotoxicity). | Commission Reg (EU) 2022/63 |
| United States (FDA) | permitted | Permitted for use as a color additive, <=1% by weight. | 21 CFR 73.575 |
| IARC (cancer-hazard classification) | not_classified | No dietary carcinogenicity classification; IARC 2B relates to inhaled TiO2 dust, not food. | IARC Monograph Vol. 93 |

## Evidence

Titanium dioxide (E171, CAS 13463-67-7) has differing regulatory statuses across jurisdictions. In the European Union, it is banned as a food additive, as it is no longer considered safe due to concerns about genotoxicity (Commission Reg (EU) 2022/63). In contrast, the United States permits its use as a color additive at levels of 1% or less by weight (21 CFR 73.575). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The safety evidence indicates that the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has conducted a re-evaluation, which raised concerns about genotoxicity (http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2016.4545). The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) has not classified titanium dioxide as a dietary carcinogen; its classification as Group 2B relates specifically to inhaled titanium dioxide dust, not food consumption (IARC Monograph Vol. 93). It is important to note that being banned in one jurisdiction or classified as a hazard does not equate to being proven harmful.
