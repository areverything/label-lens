## Evidence

Sodium benzoate (E211, CAS 532-32-1) is authorized as a preservative in the European Union, subject to conditions of use outlined in Annex II of Regulation (EC) 1333/2008. In the United States, it is permitted as a Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) antimicrobial preservative, with a usage limit not exceeding 0.1 percent (21 CFR 184.1733). The regulatory statuses differ between the two jurisdictions, with the EU having specific conditions for use while the US recognizes it as GRAS without such conditions.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has established an acceptable daily intake (ADI) for sodium benzoate at 5 mg/kg body weight per day (EFSA re-evaluation, http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2016.4433). There is no IARC classification provided for sodium benzoate, and it is important to note that a lack of a hazard classification or a ban in some jurisdictions does not equate to it being proven harmful.
