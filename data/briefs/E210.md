## Evidence

Benzoic acid (E210) is authorized as a preservative in the European Union, subject to conditions of use outlined in Annex II of Regulation (EC) 1333/2008. In the United States, it is permitted as a Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) antimicrobial preservative, with a maximum use level not exceeding 0.1 percent (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II; 21 CFR 184.1021). The regulatory statuses differ between the jurisdictions, with the EU imposing specific conditions for its use while the US recognizes it as GRAS.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has established an acceptable daily intake (ADI) for benzoic acid at 5 mg/kg body weight per day (EFSA re-evaluation, http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2016.4433). There is no IARC classification provided for benzoic acid, indicating that no cancer hazard classification is available. It is important to note that being banned in some jurisdictions or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
