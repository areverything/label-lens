## Evidence

Butylated hydroxytoluene (BHT) is authorized as an antioxidant in the European Union, subject to conditions of use outlined in Annex II of Regulation (EC) 1333/2008. In the United States, it is permitted as a Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) antioxidant, with a maximum concentration of 0.02 percent of the fat or oil content (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II; 21 CFR 182.3173). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The safety evidence indicates that BHT has been re-evaluated by the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), but specific Acceptable Daily Intake (ADI) values are not provided in the facts. The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies BHT as Group 3, meaning it is not classifiable as to carcinogenicity to humans (IARC Monograph Vol. 40). It is important to note that being classified as a hazard or banned in some jurisdictions does not equate to being proven harmful.
