# Sulfur dioxide (E220, CAS 7446-09-5)

## Identity
- **Name:** Sulfur dioxide
- **E-number:** E220
- **CAS:** 7446-09-5
- **Family:** preservative

## Regulatory status

| Jurisdiction | Status | Detail | Citation |

| European Union | authorised | Authorised sulphite preservative; foods above 10 mg/kg must be labelled 'contains sulphites'. | Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II |
| United States (FDA) | permitted | GRAS, but not permitted on meats, on recognised vitamin B1 sources, or on raw fruit and vegetables sold as fresh. | 21 CFR 182.3862 |

_No regulatory status compiled yet for: California, IARC (cancer-hazard classification)._

## Evidence

Sulfur dioxide (E220) is authorized as a preservative in the European Union, where foods containing more than 10 mg/kg must be labeled as 'contains sulphites' (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II). In the United States, it is permitted under the Generally Recognized As Safe (GRAS) designation, but it is not allowed in meats, recognized vitamin B1 sources, or raw fruits and vegetables sold as fresh (21 CFR 182.3862). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has established an acceptable daily intake (ADI) for sulfur dioxide at 0.7 mg/kg body weight per day (EFSA re-evaluation, http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2016.4438). There is no IARC classification provided for sulfur dioxide, and it is important to note that being banned in some jurisdictions or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
