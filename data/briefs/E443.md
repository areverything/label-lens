## Evidence

Brominated vegetable oil (BVO) is not authorized as a food additive in the European Union (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 (not listed)). In the United States, the FDA has revoked the authorization for BVO in food (89 FR 63765 (Aug 2024)). Additionally, California has banned brominated vegetable oil from food, effective 2027 (California AB 418 (2023)). The regulatory statuses differ between these jurisdictions, with the EU and FDA both revoking authorization, while California has implemented a future ban.

There is no safety evidence on record regarding brominated vegetable oil. Therefore, no acceptable daily intake (ADI) from EFSA or hazard classification from IARC is available. It is important to note that being banned in certain jurisdictions or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
