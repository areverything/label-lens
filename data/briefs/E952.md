## Evidence

Cyclamates (E952) are authorized as a sweetener in the European Union with specific use-level limits (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II). In contrast, they are banned for general food use in the United States, with the delisting never reversed (34 FR 17063 (1969)). This regulatory divergence highlights that while cyclamates are permitted in the EU, they remain prohibited in the U.S.

There is no safety evidence on record regarding cyclamates. Therefore, no acceptable daily intake (ADI) or IARC classification is available. It is important to note that being banned in one jurisdiction does not equate to being proven harmful, as no evidence has been provided to support claims of harm.
