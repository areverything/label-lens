## Evidence

Saccharin (E954, CAS 81-07-2) is authorized as a sweetener in the European Union, subject to conditions outlined in Annex II of Regulation (EC) 1333/2008. In the United States, it is permitted as a sweetening agent, with the previous warning-label requirement repealed in 2000 (21 CFR 180.37). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies saccharin as Group 3, meaning it is not classifiable as to carcinogenicity to humans. This classification was downgraded from Group 2B in 1999 after research indicated that the rat-bladder mechanism was not relevant to humans (IARC Monograph Vol. 73). There is no safety evidence on record regarding saccharin. It is important to note that being classified as a hazard or banned in some jurisdictions does not equate to being proven harmful.
