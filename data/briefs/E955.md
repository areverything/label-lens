## Evidence

Sucralose (E955, CAS 56038-13-2) is authorized as a sweetener in the European Union, subject to conditions of use outlined in Annex II of Regulation (EC) 1333/2008. In the United States, it is permitted as a sweetener in foods under good manufacturing practice, as specified in 21 CFR 172.831. There is no conflicting regulatory status between these jurisdictions, as both permit the use of sucralose, albeit under different regulatory frameworks.

The safety evidence includes a re-evaluation by the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), which is documented in their report (http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2016.4361). However, there is no information provided regarding an acceptable daily intake (ADI) or a classification by the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC). It is important to note that the absence of a hazard classification or a ban does not equate to being proven harmful.
