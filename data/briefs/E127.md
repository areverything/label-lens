# Erythrosine (FD&C Red 3) (E127, CAS 16423-68-0)

## Identity
- **Name:** Erythrosine (FD&C Red 3)
- **E-number:** E127
- **CAS:** 16423-68-0 (also 15905-32-5)
- **Family:** colour

## Regulatory status

| Jurisdiction | Status | Detail | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|
| European Union | authorised | Authorised food colour under Annex II conditions. | Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II |
| United States (FDA) | revoked | FDA revoked authorization for use of FD&C Red No. 3 in food and ingested drugs. | Fed. Register 90 FR 4636 (Jan 16 2025) |
| California | banned | Banned from food effective 2027 under the California Food Safety Act. | California AB 418 (2023) |

_No regulatory status compiled yet for: IARC (cancer-hazard classification)._

## Evidence

Erythrosine (FD&C Red 3) is authorized as a food color in the European Union under specific conditions (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II). In contrast, its use has been revoked in the United States by the FDA for food and ingested drugs (Fed. Register 90 FR 4636 (Jan 16 2025)). Additionally, California has banned Erythrosine from food effective 2027 under the California Food Safety Act (California AB 418 (2023)). This regulatory divergence highlights that while Erythrosine is permitted in the EU, it is prohibited in the US and California.

The safety evidence includes a re-evaluation by the European Food Safety Authority (EFSA), which is documented (http://dx.doi.org/10.2903/j.efsa.2011.1854). However, no specific Acceptable Daily Intake (ADI) or IARC classification is provided in the facts. It is important to note that being banned in certain jurisdictions or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
