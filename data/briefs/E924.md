## Evidence

Potassium bromate (E924, CAS 7758-01-2) has differing regulatory statuses across jurisdictions. In the European Union and the UK, it is not authorised as a food additive (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 (not listed)). In the United States, it is permitted as a flour treatment agent, although the FDA has urged voluntary discontinuation (21 CFR 136 / 137). In California, it is banned from food effective 2027 (California AB 418 (2023)). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

Regarding safety evidence, the International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies potassium bromate as 'possibly carcinogenic to humans' (Group 2B) (IARC Monograph Vol. 73). However, there is no evidence on record to provide further details on safety or acceptable daily intake (ADI) levels. It is important to note that being banned in some places or having a hazard classification does not equate to being proven harmful.
