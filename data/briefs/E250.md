## Evidence

Sodium nitrite (E250) is authorized as a preservative in the European Union, with maximum added amounts being lowered by Regulation (EU) 2023/2108, effective from October 2025 (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II (amended by Reg (EU) 2023/2108)). In the United States, it is permitted as a preservative and color fixative in cured and smoked meat and fish, with specific limits set (21 CFR 172.175). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has conducted a re-evaluation of sodium nitrite, but specific safety evidence such as an Acceptable Daily Intake (ADI) is not provided in the facts. The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies ingested nitrate or nitrite under conditions that result in endogenous nitrosation as probably carcinogenic (group 2A), but this classification pertains to the exposure condition rather than the additive itself (IARC Monograph Vol. 94). It is important to note that a hazard classification does not equate to proven harm.
