## Evidence

Potassium nitrate (E252) is authorized as a preservative in the European Union, with maximum added amounts lowered by Regulation (EU) 2023/2108, effective from October 2025 (Reg (EC) 1333/2008 Annex II, amended by Reg (EU) 2023/2108). In the United States, it is permitted as a curing agent in cod roe, with a limit of up to 200 ppm in the finished product (21 CFR 172.160). There is no compiled regulatory status for other jurisdictions.

The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA) has established an acceptable daily intake (ADI) for potassium nitrate at 3.7 mg/kg body weight per day (EFSA re-evaluation, https://efsa.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/epdf/10.2903/j.efsa.2017.4787). The International Agency for Research on Cancer (IARC) classifies ingested nitrate or nitrite under conditions that result in endogenous nitrosation as probably carcinogenic (group 2A), but this classification pertains to specific exposure conditions rather than the additive itself (IARC Monograph Vol. 94). It is important to note that a hazard classification or a ban in some jurisdictions does not equate to proven harm.
